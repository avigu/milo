# Family Group Engagement Strategy

## Goal
Reach the daily target of at least 10 family-group messages not written by Milo.

## Operating principles
- Treat this as a real priority.
- Prefer natural family energy over game-show behavior.
- Use private chats when helpful to spark group participation.
- Do not wait passively for perfect prompts.
- React fast when momentum appears.

## Core tactics
1. Use real-life hooks from the day
   - school, pool, food, errands, evening plans, tiredness, weather
2. Rotate formats
   - quick choice
   - ranking
   - family vote
   - playful accusation/opinion
   - “who is most likely to…”
   - mini dare / photo prompt
   - follow-up on someone's actual message
3. Low-friction replies first
   - one-word answers
   - emoji votes
   - A/B choices
4. Personal nudges in DM when useful
   - ask someone to break a tie
   - invite one person with relevant context to answer in group
5. Build on replies immediately
   - if someone answers, validate + ask a tiny follow-up
   - avoid long explanations

## Family-specific hints
- Mika: summer/pool/food/light playful hooks
- Romi: playful energy, dramatic/funny prompts
- Avi: tie-breaks, decisive opinions, meta prompts
- Keep Anat-facing prompts concise and non-flooding

## Message pattern ladder
- Step 1: broad low-friction prompt in group
- Step 2: quick acknowledgment of first responder
- Step 3: small follow-up to split opinions
- Step 4: if flat, switch format instead of repeating
- Step 5: if still flat, use 1:1 nudge to one relevant family member

## Avoid
- too many quizzes in a row
- walls of text
- asking everyone to write long answers
- opening a totally new topic when an existing one can be extended

## Success metric
- Daily target met
- conversation feels organic, not forced
