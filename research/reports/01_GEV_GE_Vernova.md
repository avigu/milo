## 1. Company Overview

**GE Vernova** was spun off from General Electric in April 2024 and is now the world's most comprehensive energy technology company by breadth. It sits at the intersection of gas power, wind energy, and grid electrification — three of the most critical themes in the AI/datacenter buildout.

**Business Segments:**
| Segment | Revenue Mix | Margin Profile |
|---------|------------|----------------|
| Power (gas turbines, steam, services) | ~55% | High — 15–20% operating margin |
| Wind (onshore + offshore) | ~25% | Improving — approaching breakeven |
| Electrification (grid, transformers, software) | ~20% | High growth, expanding margins |

**Revenue TTM:** ~$39.4B  
**Main Customers:** Utilities (Duke, NextEra, Dominion), IPPs (Vistra, Constellation), independent generators, hyperscaler supply chain (indirectly via PPAs for gas/wind plants)  
**Geographic Exposure:** ~55% North America, ~25% Europe, ~20% Rest of World

## 2. Strategic Position

GEV is a **critical infrastructure bottleneck provider**. Here's why:

- **Gas turbines:** Only 3 companies globally make large gas turbines: GE Vernova, Siemens Energy, Mitsubishi Power. With AI-driven power demand causing a surge in gas power plant orders, this oligopoly has extraordinary pricing power. Lead times are now 3–4 years. GEV holds a leading position in the U.S. and globally.
- **Grid transformers:** Among the most constrained components in the grid buildout. GEV's Electrification segment makes power transformers and grid automation equipment with 1–2 year lead times.
- **Wind turbines:** The Haliade-X offshore turbine is among the world's largest (14–18 MW); onshore Cypress platform is globally competitive.

**Replaceability:** Essentially impossible in the near/medium term. Customers can't easily switch gas turbine vendors mid-project — the entire plant is engineered around one supplier's equipment.

**Strategic Criticality:** 10/10. GEV is the power behind the AI infrastructure boom, literally.

## 3. AI / Datacenter Exposure

**Direct exposure:** Very high. The AI datacenter buildout requires:
1. New gas power plants (GEV turbines) to power datacenters 24/7
2. Grid infrastructure upgrades (GEV Electrification) to deliver that power
3. Wind farms (GEV Wind) for hyperscaler renewable PPAs

**Hyperscaler exposure:** Indirect but deep. Microsoft, Amazon, Google and others are signing PPAs with utilities and IPPs who are ordering GEV turbines. The Microsoft/Three Mile Island restart and new gas plant orders are creating a multi-year turbine ordering cycle.

**AI as business driver:** Real, not just narrative. GEV's Power order intake grew >30% in 2025. The company guided for revenue growth and significant margin expansion.

**Estimated AI-driven growth contribution:** ~40–50% of new gas turbine orders are driven by datacenter power demand.

## 6. Bottlenecks & Capacity Constraints

**Benefits from scarcity:** YES — strongly.

- Gas turbine backlog: ~$115B+ (>2.5 years of revenue)
- Order-to-delivery: 3–4 years
- GEV cannot simply add capacity overnight (casting foundries, specialized workforce)
- Every additional order entrenches pricing power further

**Supply chain constraints:** Forging and casting (blades, rotors) are the binding constraints. GEV is investing in manufacturing expansion but deliberately limiting output growth to protect margins.

**Demand > Supply:** Yes, definitively. The industry has not built meaningful new capacity in 15+ years (post-2008 order drought). Now demand has returned explosively.

## 9. Regulatory & Political Risk

- **IRA (Inflation Reduction Act):** Positive — clean energy manufacturing credits support GEV's wind and grid business
- **Gas power plants:** Political risk from carbon regulation, but AI-driven demand makes politicians much more supportive of gas as a "bridge"
- **Export controls:** Risk if geopolitical tensions restrict turbine exports to certain markets
- **Trade tariffs:** Some steel/component cost risk; GEV is largely U.S.-manufactured
- **Rate regulation:** Indirect — utilities pass GEV equipment costs through regulated rate base

**Overall political risk:** Moderate and manageable. GEV is essential infrastructure regardless of administration.
