## #1 — Constellation Energy (CEG) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ STRONG BUY

**Price:** $303.63 | **Target:** $375 | **Upside:** +24% + dividend

### Why #1

Constellation Energy is the single most irreplaceable asset in the AI/electrification era. Here is the core thesis in three sentences:

> *AI hyperscalers need 24/7 carbon-free power. Nuclear is the only technology that can deliver it at scale. Constellation is the largest U.S. nuclear operator with 21 GW of existing, licensed, operational capacity that cannot be replicated.*

The Microsoft Three Mile Island deal ($100/MWh, 20 years) was a watershed moment — the largest tech company in the world paying a significant premium for nuclear power because AI data centers cannot run on intermittent wind and solar. Amazon, Google, Meta will follow. CEG has only signed one major tech PPA so far; the next 2-3 are likely in negotiation.

**Why it pulled back from $412 to $303:**
- 2024 saw abnormally high energy prices → 2025 EPS normalized → YoY EPS decline of -37.7%
- Market read this as deterioration; it was normalization from an exceptional base
- The forward P/E of 25.4x reflects a normalized, lower earnings base — not a broken company

## #2 — Cheniere Energy (LNG) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ STRONG BUY

**Price:** $240.11 | **Target:** $303 | **Upside:** +26%

### Why #2

Cheniere is the most mispriced security in this basket. At 9.5x trailing P/E and 7.0x EV/EBITDA, it trades at a significant discount to any reasonable comparator — despite having:
- 80%+ of revenue under take-or-pay contracts
- 45.6% operating margins
- 79% ROE
- Nearly zero correlation to the broader market (beta: 0.056)
- 26% upside to analyst consensus target

The AI/electrification thesis: Gas remains the backbone of U.S. electricity generation because solar and wind can't run 24/7. More AI data centers = more gas-fired generation = more gas demand = higher domestic prices → tighter global supply → higher LNG prices → Cheniere earns more on spot volumes above its contracted floor.

The recent Q4 2025 massive beat ($10.68 actual vs $3.94 estimate) shows what happens when LNG spot prices spike — Cheniere converts that to earnings rapidly.

## #3 — EQT Corporation (EQT) ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ STRONG BUY

**Price:** $55.96 | **Target:** $70.48 | **Upside:** +26%

### Why #3

EQT is the cheapest way to invest in the AI gas demand supercycle. As the largest U.S. natural gas producer, EQT benefits directly from every incremental power demand increase driven by AI data centers.

The numbers are compelling: 5.5x EV/EBITDA, 10.7x P/E TTM, 26% upside to analyst target, 49.7% operating margins, and quarterly earnings beats. The stock has been flat for a year (+4.8%) despite dramatically improving fundamentals — the market hasn't caught up.

The Equitrans acquisition (2024) was a strategic masterstroke: EQT now owns its own pipelines, reducing all-in production costs by ~$0.30–0.40/MMBtu — directly expanding margins.

Every $0.50 increase in natural gas prices (Henry Hub) translates to ~$1-2B additional EBITDA for EQT. With AI-driven gas demand growing 5-8% annually, the structural gas price floor is rising.

## Macro Risks to the Entire Thesis

1. **AI capex slowdown** — If Microsoft/Amazon/Google cut capex, demand for turbines, data centers, and grid equipment falls. Probability: 15% in next 18 months.
2. **Interest rate spike** — Hurts utilities (NEE, DUK, CEG) and REITs (EQIX, DLR) via higher discount rates and financing costs.
3. **Natural gas price crash** — If LNG global oversupply materializes, EQT and LNG suffer. Probability: 20% in 2026.
4. **Nuclear incident** — Tail risk that could devastate CEG and CCJ. Probability: Very low (<1% per year per reactor).
5. **Regulatory reversal on IRA** — If clean energy tax credits are cut, renewable development slows. GEV, NEE partially exposed.
