# U.S. Electricity Generation & Power Infrastructure — Value Chain Analysis
*Prepared: May 2026 | Analyst: Milo (AI Research)*

---

## Executive Summary

The U.S. electricity sector is undergoing its most profound transformation since electrification itself. Three simultaneous forces — AI/datacenter demand explosion, energy transition, and grid modernization — are creating a multi-decade, multi-trillion-dollar investment supercycle. Electricity demand in the U.S. was expected to be flat through 2030; it is now forecast to grow 15–20% by 2030 and potentially double by 2050. This has made every layer of the electricity value chain strategically critical and investable.

---

## Layer 1: Energy Sources

### 1A. Natural Gas
**Role:** The backbone of U.S. power generation (~40% of electricity). Gas-fired plants provide the fastest-dispatching firm power — critical for grid stability as intermittent renewables grow.

**Key Technologies:** Combined cycle gas turbines (CCGT), peaker plants, LNG export terminals

**Bottleneck:** Pipeline capacity, LNG export constraints, methane regulations

**AI Impact:** AI data centers demand 24/7 firm power → gas plants remain essential as backup and baseload; LNG export creates price link between U.S. and global markets

**Moat:** Long-term supply contracts, pipeline infrastructure, regulatory permits

**Key Companies:** EQT (upstream gas), Cheniere (LNG export), Williams Companies, Kinder Morgan

---

### 1B. Nuclear
**Role:** ~18% of U.S. electricity, but >50% of zero-carbon electricity. Nuclear provides always-on baseload power — exactly what hyperscalers want for datacenter PPAs.

**Key Technologies:** PWR/BWR reactors (existing fleet), SMRs (emerging), advanced Gen IV

**Bottleneck:** Uranium supply chain, skilled workforce, regulatory approvals, construction costs

**AI Impact:** Microsoft (Three Mile Island PPA with CEG), Amazon, Google signing nuclear PPAs → direct demand driver for nuclear operators and uranium miners

**Moat:** Plant licenses (decades-long), fuel supply agreements, zero-carbon credentials

**Key Companies:** Constellation Energy (largest U.S. nuclear operator), Cameco (uranium fuel)

---

### 1C. Renewables (Solar & Wind)
**Role:** Fastest-growing source; solar+wind now ~15% of U.S. generation and rising

**Key Technologies:** Utility-scale solar PV, onshore/offshore wind, floating offshore wind

**Bottleneck:** Grid interconnection queues (years-long delays), land permitting, transmission

**AI Impact:** Hyperscalers signing massive PPAs (Amazon 100+ GW commitment); renewables become the energy source, not the dispatch source → storage/gas fill the gaps

**Moat:** Land rights, interconnection agreements, long-term PPAs, manufacturing scale (panels/blades)

**Key Companies:** NextEra Energy (largest wind/solar operator in U.S.)

---

### 1D. Uranium / Nuclear Fuel
**Role:** Fuel for existing 93 U.S. nuclear reactors and growing fleet

**Key Technologies:** In situ recovery (ISR), conventional mining, conversion, enrichment, fuel fabrication

**Bottleneck:** Post-Fukushima underinvestment → structural global supply deficit; Russian ban on enriched uranium imports

**AI Impact:** Nuclear renaissance driven by AI datacenter demand → uranium demand structurally higher

**Key Companies:** Cameco (CCJ), Kazatomprom (KAP), Uranium Energy Corp (UEC), Energy Fuels (UUUU)

## Layer 2: Power Generation Equipment

### 2A. Gas Turbines
**Role:** Heart of gas-fired power plants; multi-year lead times creating bottleneck

**Key Technologies:** H-class, F-class, HA turbines (GE, Siemens, Mitsubishi); aeroderivative turbines

**Bottleneck:** 3–4 year order backlogs; casting/forging capacity limited; skilled labor

**AI Impact:** Every new datacenter campus needs dedicated generation → turbine orders soaring

**Moat:** Few manufacturers globally (GE Vernova, Siemens Energy, Mitsubishi Power), decades of IP, service contracts on installed base

**Margin:** High on services/parts; growing on equipment as pricing power returns

**Key Companies:** GE Vernova (GEV), Siemens Energy (SMNEY)

---

### 2B. Power Transformers
**Role:** Step up/step down voltage for transmission/distribution; critical grid component

**Key Technologies:** Large power transformers (LPT), distribution transformers; solid-state transformers (emerging)

**Bottleneck:** U.S. has ~80% import dependency; 1–2+ year lead times; raw material constraints (steel, copper)

**AI Impact:** Grid expansion for AI infrastructure → transformer demand surge

**Moat:** Very few domestic manufacturers; high custom engineering; long backlogs

**Key Companies:** ABB (ABB), Eaton (ETN), Siemens Energy, Hitachi Energy

---

### 2C. Switchgear & Electrical Components
**Role:** Control, protect, and isolate electrical circuits; critical in data centers and substations

**Key Technologies:** Medium voltage switchgear, bus ducts, circuit breakers, PDUs

**Bottleneck:** Copper shortages; production capacity

**AI Impact:** Every AI data center requires extensive switchgear; hyperscaler orders growing

**Moat:** Certifications, long customer relationships, complex integration

**Key Companies:** Eaton (ETN), ABB (ABB), Schneider Electric (SBGSY)

---

## Layer 3: Grid Infrastructure

### 3A. Transmission & Distribution
**Role:** Moving power from generation to end user; aging U.S. grid requires massive investment

**Key Technologies:** HVDC lines, underground cables, smart grid sensors, grid-scale automation

**Bottleneck:** Permitting (years for new lines), material shortages, skilled labor

**AI Impact:** Load growth from AI data centers in specific geographies creates local grid stress

**Moat:** Regulated utility monopoly, physical right-of-way, regulatory relationships

**Key Companies:** Duke Energy (DUK), NextEra Energy (NEE), Quanta Services (PWR — EPC)

---

### 3B. Grid EPC & Engineering
**Role:** Design, build, and maintain electrical infrastructure

**Key Technologies:** Substation construction, underground cabling, line upgrades

**Bottleneck:** Labor shortage (linemen, electricians), equipment lead times

**AI Impact:** AI-driven demand = massive new transmission/substation build → EPC backlog explosion

**Moat:** Scale, relationships, specialized labor, safety records

**Key Companies:** Quanta Services (PWR), MYR Group (MYRG), Mastec (MTZ)

---

## Layer 4: Power Generation Operators

### 4A. Independent Power Producers (IPPs)
**Role:** Own and operate generation assets; sell power to utilities or directly via PPA

**Bottleneck:** Generation shortage in high-load regions; permitting for new capacity

**AI Impact:** Direct beneficiaries — hyperscalers signing long-term PPAs with IPPs

**Moat:** Existing generation assets, fuel contracts, long-term offtake agreements

**Key Companies:** Vistra (VST), NRG Energy, Calpine

---

## Layer 5: Utilities

### 5A. Integrated Utilities
**Role:** Vertically integrated — own generation, transmission, distribution; serve retail customers

**Bottleneck:** Regulatory approval cycles, rate case timing, capital access

**AI Impact:** Data center customers are becoming largest single loads; rate cases reflect new demand

**Moat:** Regulated monopoly franchise, guaranteed rate of return on capex

**Key Companies:** Duke Energy (DUK), NextEra Energy (NEE), Constellation Energy (CEG — deregulated nuclear operator/utility hybrid)

---

## Layer 6: Datacenter Power & Cooling

### 6A. Power Management & UPS
**Role:** Ensure 99.9999% uptime in data centers; handle power conversion, conditioning, backup

**Key Technologies:** UPS systems, PDUs, bus bars, RPPs, modular power systems

**Bottleneck:** Lead times for UPS/switchgear; hyperscaler spec changes

**AI Impact:** Direct AI beneficiary — every GPU cluster needs power management

**Moat:** Hyperscaler qualification, integrated service, installed base

**Key Companies:** Vertiv (VRT), Eaton (ETN), Schneider Electric (SBGSY)

---

### 6B. Thermal Management / Cooling
**Role:** Remove heat from GPU clusters; critical as AI chip power density increases

**Key Technologies:** Air cooling (CRAC/CRAH), liquid cooling (cold plate, immersion), rear-door heat exchangers

**Bottleneck:** Liquid cooling manufacturing scale; GPU density requires redesigned thermal systems

**AI Impact:** H100 = 700W; Blackwell = 1000W+ → liquid cooling mandatory for dense AI clusters

**Moat:** NVIDIA qualification, datacenter integration, service relationships

**Key Companies:** Vertiv (VRT), Schneider Electric (SBGSY), Modine, CoolIT

---

### 6C. Datacenter REITs
**Role:** Own and operate datacenter facilities; lease space and power to cloud/hyperscaler tenants

**Key Technologies:** Campus-scale facilities, hyperscale leasing, interconnection fabric

**Bottleneck:** Power availability (hardest constraint), land, construction labor

**AI Impact:** AI inference/training requires massive datacenter footprint; hyperscalers leasing aggressively

**Moat:** Campus scale, power access, interconnection density, relationships

**Key Companies:** Equinix (EQIX), Digital Realty (DLR), Iron Mountain (IRM)

---

## Layer 7: Energy Storage

### 7A. Battery Energy Storage Systems (BESS)
**Role:** Store excess renewable energy; provide grid stability, frequency regulation, capacity

**Key Technologies:** Li-ion (LFP dominant), flow batteries, sodium-ion (emerging)

**Bottleneck:** Lithium/LFP cell supply, inverter availability, interconnection

**AI Impact:** AI load creates volatile demand patterns → storage becomes grid-balancing asset

**Moat:** Scale, software optimization, site permits, utility relationships

**Key Companies:** Fluence Energy (FLNC), Tesla Energy (TSLA), Stem, NextEra Energy Partners

## Layer 9: AI-Driven Electricity Demand

**The Demand Shock:** AI training and inference are extraordinarily power-hungry:
- ChatGPT query: ~10× more energy than Google search
- A 100,000 GPU H100 cluster: ~500MW constant load
- Projected U.S. datacenter power demand: ~30 GW (2023) → ~80-130 GW (2030)

**Hyperscaler Spending:**
- Microsoft: $80B capex 2025, secured nuclear PPAs (Three Mile Island via CEG)
- Amazon: 100+ GW renewable PPAs committed through 2030
- Google: investing in nuclear SMRs, offshore wind
- Meta: $60–65B capex 2025

**Grid Impact:** This demand is largely concentrated in Northern Virginia (Loudoun County — 70% of world's internet traffic), Phoenix, Dallas, Chicago, Atlanta — creating acute local grid stress.

## Key Cross-Layer Dependencies

```
AI/Hyperscaler Demand
        ↓
Datacenter REITs (EQIX, DLR) — need power
        ↓
Power Management (VRT, ETN, SBGSY) — need reliable power
        ↓
Generation (CEG, VST, NEE nuclear/gas/solar)
        ↓
Fuel (CCJ uranium, EQT/LNG gas)
        ↓
Equipment (GEV turbines, ETN/ABB transformers)
        ↓
Grid EPC (PWR builds the connections)
        ↓
Grid Operators (DUK, NEE utilities)
        ↓
Storage (FLNC, TSLA balance the load)
```

## Key Investment Themes for 2026–2030

1. **Turbine scarcity premium**: GEV, SMNEY benefit from order backlogs
2. **Nuclear renaissance**: CEG (operator), CCJ (fuel) are pure-play beneficiaries
3. **Grid bottleneck**: PWR (EPC), ETN/ABB (transformers) benefit from transmission build
4. **AI power management**: VRT is the most direct AI beneficiary in power
5. **LNG/gas bridge**: LNG and EQT benefit as gas remains the baseload fuel
6. **Utility defensive growth**: NEE, DUK benefit from regulated growth + AI demand
