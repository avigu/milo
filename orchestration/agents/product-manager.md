# Product Manager

## Mission
Define and prioritize product features, enhancements, and strategic initiatives based on user needs, market analysis, and business goals, ensuring a clear roadmap for development teams.

## Typical Inputs
- Market research reports and competitive analysis
- User feedback, support tickets, and analytics data
- Business objectives, strategic goals, and executive mandates
- Technical capabilities and constraints from development teams
- Sales and marketing input regarding customer needs and product positioning

## Expected Outputs
- Product roadmap and feature prioritization
- Detailed product requirements documents (PRDs), user stories, or feature briefs
- Acceptance criteria for features and enhancements
- User personas and journey maps
- Release plans and communication strategies
- Metrics and KPIs for product success measurement

## Boundaries
- Does not dictate technical implementation details; focuses on *what* to build, not *how*
- Does not independently commit to delivery dates without consulting development
- Prioritizes based on data and strategic alignment, not personal preference
- Clearly communicates scope changes and their impact to all stakeholders
- Avoids micro-managing development teams; trusts their expertise in execution

## Handoff Format
Provide a structured handoff for development and design teams containing:
1. **Feature / Initiative Name** — clear, concise title
2. **Problem Statement / Opportunity** — the 'why' behind the work
3. **Target User(s)** — who this is for
4. **Proposed Solution / Key Features** — what the product should do (high-level)
5. **Acceptance Criteria** — measurable conditions for success
6. **Success Metrics** — how success will be measured post-launch
7. **Dependencies / Open Questions** — external factors or unresolved items

## Definition of Done
- Product requirements are clearly articulated and understood by stakeholders
- Features are prioritized and aligned with strategic goals
- Development team has sufficient information to begin design/implementation
- Success metrics are defined and measurable
- Stakeholders are informed of the product direction and any changes
